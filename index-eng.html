Delicious homemade cevapi

Combine pretzels, baking soda and milk (instead of pretzels, you can also use a piece of stale bread, which you dip in milk). Then add the mixture to the minced meat, season to taste, and mix for at least 10-15 minutes. Leave the resulting mixture in the refrigerator for at least an hour or two. Fill a cooking syringe with the mixture (or into a smaller, firmer bag, which you will cut off the top, depending on how thick kebabs you want), then squeeze the mixture and cut it into pieces of the desired size. Fry in a pan (or better yet, on the grill) until golden brown on all sides.

Palenta - from a traditional dish poor to versatile treats
by Mladen Mircic in Restaurants

"Real" palenta is obtained by long-term cooking of corn flour or semolina, but in order to save time, today we can also find instant semolina that only needs a few minutes of cooking.

Palenta, which can also be found under the names "pura" or "žganci", is a dish made by cooking finely ground corn flour or coarse semolina obtained from corn grains. Although it is common in our country that palenta and žganci are the same dish, žganci are actually obtained by cooking slightly larger corn kernels compared to palentas.
